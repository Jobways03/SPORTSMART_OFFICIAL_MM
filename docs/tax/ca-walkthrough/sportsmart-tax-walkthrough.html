1. The 10-second mental model

Sportsmart is an electronic-commerce marketplace: independent sellers list products; customers buy them; Sportsmart handles the storefront, payments, fulfilment, and statutory paperwork. Three statutory things happen on every sale:

WhatWho paysWho collects/deductsWhere it ends up
GST on the product (CGST + SGST or IGST)CustomerThe seller (we issue the invoice on their behalf)Seller's GSTR-1 / GSTR-3B
TCS @ 1% (Section 52)The seller (deducted from settlement)Sportsmart (marketplace)Our GSTR-8
TDS @ 1% (Section 194-O Income Tax)The seller (deducted from settlement)Sportsmart (marketplace)Our Form 26Q + Form 16A

In addition, when Sportsmart earns commission from a seller, that commission is itself a service supply from Sportsmart to the seller under SAC 9985 — Sportsmart charges 18% GST on it and reports it on its own GSTR-1.

Why this is complex: for every order we have to (a) issue a GST-compliant invoice for the seller, (b) deduct TCS, (c) deduct TDS, (d) charge GST on our own commission, (e) handle returns within Section-34 time limits, and (f) report all of this in monthly/quarterly returns. The code automates all six and exposes admin pages to file the resulting statements.

2. The four parties on every order

  1. Customer — the buyer. May be B2C (no GSTIN) or B2B (registered, with one or more GSTINs).
  2. Supplier — the entity that is legally selling the goods. One of three kinds:
    • MARKETPLACE_SELLER — independent seller on the platform
    • FRANCHISE — franchise partner
    • SPORTSMART — Sportsmart-direct supplies
  3. Marketplace — Sportsmart itself. For seller and franchise supplies, the marketplace is an operator under Section 52 (TCS) and a person responsible for payment under Section 194-O (TDS). For SPORTSMART-direct supplies, the marketplace IS the supplier.
  4. Government — CBIC for GST; CBDT for Income-Tax TDS. Both consume statutory returns that the admin team downloads from this system and files.

3. How the GST math runs on a line

Every cart line goes through a single pure function (file: apps/api/src/modules/tax/domain/tax-engine.ts). Inputs:

  • Gross amount (paise) — the price × quantity
  • Discount (paise) — already allocated to this line
  • GST rate (basis points; 1800 = 18%)
  • Cess rate (basis points; default 0)
  • Whether price includes tax (B2C convention: yes) or excludes tax
  • Intra-state? (true → CGST + SGST; false → IGST)
  • Supply taxability (TAXABLE, NIL_RATED, EXEMPT, NON_GST, ZERO_RATED, OUT_OF_SCOPE)

Output (per line):

{ taxableInPaise, cgstInPaise, sgstInPaise, igstInPaise,
  cessInPaise, totalTaxInPaise, lineTotalInPaise }

Invariants the engine enforces

  • cgst + sgst + igst === totalTax
  • taxable + totalTax + cess === lineTotal (no penny ever lost in rounding)
  • For intra-state: CGST = SGST = half of total GST (with SGST derived as totalTax − CGST so the sum is exact)

Rounding

Each component is computed in BigInt paise using "half away from zero" rounding. The invoice grand total is rounded to the nearest whole rupee, and the difference (positive or negative) is shown as a Round-off line in line with CBIC convention.

Inclusive vs exclusive pricing

ModeUsed forHow the engine handles it
GST-inclusive (default for B2C catalog)Storefront retail pricesThe displayed MRP already includes tax. The engine "backs out" the tax: taxable = price ÷ (1 + rate), tax = price − taxable.
GST-exclusiveB2B / wholesaleTax is added on top: taxable = price, tax = price × rate.

4. Place of Supply — intra-state vs inter-state

Indian GST splits a supply's tax based on where the recipient is, relative to the supplier:

  • Same state (supplier state = customer state) → CGST + SGST (half / half)
  • Different state → IGST (full rate)

How each party's state is determined

PartySource of the 2-digit GST state code
Customer (B2C)Shipping address. State name resolved against the 38-row india_states CBIC master, OR the new state_code column on the address (Phase 34 — dropdown-driven).
Customer (B2B)Same as B2C — POS source is the shipping address state, NOT the billing/GSTIN state (assumption #44 in the decision log; CA to confirm).
SellerSeller's primary GSTIN's state (first 2 digits of the 15-char GSTIN).
FranchiseFranchise's registered GSTIN state.
SPORTSMARTplatform_gst_profiles.gstStateCode for the row marked isDefault=true.
CA confirm: for B2B orders where the customer has supplied a GSTIN, IGST Act §12(2)(a) is being interpreted to mean POS = recipient's shipping state. If your interpretation requires POS = recipient's registered state, this is a flag to change.

5. Discounts and how they interact with GST

Indian GST law (CGST Act Section 15) requires discount to be applied before GST when it's a pre-supply transactional discount established at the time of supply. The engine treats coupon/promo discounts this way by default. Three discount kinds are distinguished:

KindTax treatmentExample
PRE_SUPPLY_TRANSACTIONALReduces taxable value at sale time. Lower taxable → lower GST.Coupon "FLAT200"
POST_SUPPLY_LINKEDAdjusted after sale via a Credit Note linked to original invoice. Reverses GST.Quarterly volume rebate
POST_SUPPLY_UNLINKEDNo GST reversal possible (Section 34 ineligible). Booked as wallet credit / goodwill.Ad-hoc admin write-off
DISPLAY_ONLYJust an MRP slash for marketing. Taxable value = the actual price charged."₹2,499 / ₹3,499"

Document numbering

Per-(GSTIN, financial year) sequence. Format examples:

  • Tax Invoice: SM-INV-000001
  • Bill of Supply: SM-BOS-000001
  • Credit Note: SM-CN-000001
  • Debit Note: SM-DN-000001

Max 16 chars per CBIC rule. Allowed characters: A-Z 0-9 / -. Sequence is locked at the database level (Postgres SERIALIZABLE transaction on the document_sequences table) so two parallel writes can never get the same number. Cancelled numbers are kept in skippedNumbers — never reused.

Within the IRN window (24 hours after IRN, when e-invoicing is live): cancel through NIC IRP. After the window or for non-IRN documents: no in-place cancellation — issue a Credit Note (full reversal) and a fresh Invoice instead. Cancelled document numbers are preserved on the row and never reused.

7. Section 52 — TCS (1%)

As an e-commerce operator, the marketplace collects Tax Collected at Source @ 1% on the net taxable supplies of every marketplace seller (and franchise) it processes. This is paid into the Government's GST account against the seller's GSTIN.

8. Section 194-O — Income-Tax TDS (1%)

A separate statute (Income-Tax Act, not GST). Section 194-O makes the e-commerce operator the person responsible for deducting TDS @ 1% on the gross sale value (including GST) paid through the marketplace to a participating seller.

TopicBehaviour
Rate1% of gross sale value (incl. GST), 5% if seller has no PAN
Threshold for individual/HUF sellers₹5 lakh aggregate per FY — sellers below get exempted via an admin attestation flag (seller.is194OExempt)
TimingAt settlement payout to the seller
ReturnForm 26Q quarterly (CSV download; admin files via TIN-FC or e-filing)
Certificate to sellerForm 16A quarterly (HTML page; admin "Print → Save as PDF")
LifecyclePENDING → DEPOSITED → CERTIFICATE_ISSUED via the admin TDS dashboard
Settlement payout to the seller = (gross order value) − (commission + commission GST) − (TCS deducted) − (TDS deducted). All four deductions appear on the seller's payout statement.

9. Section 34 — Credit Notes and the 30-September time-bar

CGST Act Section 34(2) lets a supplier reduce their output GST liability by issuing a Credit Note only if the credit note is declared in a return filed on or before:

30 September of the financial year FOLLOWING the year of supply.
Example: an invoice issued on 12 January 2026 (FY 2025-26) can be credit-noted up to 30 September 2026.

Returns approved after this cutoff cannot reduce GST — the platform absorbs the GST cost and the customer is refunded via wallet credit instead. A daily cron (tax_credit_note_timebar.cron.ts) classifies every QC-approved return into one of three buckets:

StatusMeaningWhat happens
ELIGIBLEInside the Section-34 windowSystem issues a Credit Note + reverses GST on filings
TIME_BARREDPast 30 Sept of FY+1No GST reversal. Refund via wallet credit. Customer-facing line: "Refund processed. GST adjustment is not available for this return due to statutory reporting timelines."
REQUIRES_FINANCE_REVIEWBorderline (within 7 days of cutoff, or unusual invoice state)Lands on the admin Time-bar Review queue. A finance lead chooses Credit Note OR wallet credit manually.

10. Returns — refund / credit note / wallet

Every customer return that passes QC falls into one of three refund paths, chosen by the eligibility classifier above:

  1. Real Credit Note — reduces the seller's output GST in the filing period the CN is declared. The system writes a CREDIT_NOTE document (own number, own number sequence) linked to the original invoice. Customer refund + GST reversal both happen.
  2. Wallet adjustment — when CN is not eligible (time-barred, unlinked discount, goodwill, etc.), a wallet credit is issued. No GST is reversed. Two-step admin approval is required when the amount exceeds the configured threshold (goodwill_approval_threshold_paise).
  3. Mixed — partial returns where some lines are CN-eligible and others are wallet-only.

The customer's return-detail page now shows both: a Credit Note card with the CN number/date/amount, and a Wallet Credit card with the wallet ledger ID and reason.

14. Form 26Q and Form 16A in detail

Form 26Q is the quarterly TDS return filed by every TDS deductor under the Income Tax Act. Form 16A is the TDS certificate issued to each deductee (each seller) for the same quarter.

The TDS lifecycle the admin runs

  1. System auto-creates a section_194o_tds_ledger row per (seller, quarter) at settlement.
  2. Status starts as PENDING.
  3. Admin downloads the Form 26Q CSV from /dashboard/tax/tds194o, files via TIN-FC, and clicks Mark DEPOSITED. Status → DEPOSITED, with a UTR reference saved.
  4. Admin then generates Form 16A per ledger row (one per seller-quarter), prints to PDF, and emails the seller. Status → CERTIFICATE_ISSUED.

Form 16A — what's on the certificate

  • Deductor (Sportsmart) name, address, TAN, PAN
  • Deductee (seller) name, address, PAN, GSTIN
  • Section 194-O statement; quarter (Q1 / Q2 / Q3 / Q4); FY
  • Sale value facilitated under §194-O (gross)
  • TDS deducted (1% of gross)
  • TDS deposited date + challan reference (UTR)
  • Verification by deductor

15. The marketplace's OWN GSTR-1 commission section

Distinct from the per-seller GSTR-1 above. When Sportsmart earns commission from a seller, that commission is a service supply by Sportsmart to the seller — taxable under SAC 9985 @ 18%.

The export at /dashboard/tax (Marketplace GSTR-1 commission section) returns a CSV grouped per (seller GSTIN, state) for the filing period:

  • Recipient GSTIN (the seller)
  • Recipient legal name
  • Recipient state code
  • Filing Period
  • SAC Code (9985)
  • Rate (18%)
  • Taxable Value (commission in rupees)
  • CGST, SGST, IGST
  • Total GST
  • Tax split (CGST_SGST or IGST)
  • Number of constituent settlements

Admin imports this CSV into Sportsmart's own GSTR-1 filing, alongside the per-seller §4 / §7 sections.

21. Retention and erasure

CGST Act Section 36 — retain books / records for at least 72 months from the end of the FY of the supply. Our defaults match:

Record classRetention
Tax documents (invoices, credit notes, BoS)72 months
PDFs72 months
TCS + TDS ledgers72 months
E-way bills72 months
Tax-related audit log72 months
Wallet adjustments (non-tax goodwill)24 months

GDPR / DPDP right-to-erasure: tax records are excluded from delete. The system may anonymise PII (name, contact) on the row but the GSTIN, document number, amounts, and tax columns are statutorily retained.

23. Quick CA-meeting checklist

Bring this to the meeting and walk down the list. Each item is an engineering assumption that the system already runs on — the CA's job is to confirm or correct it before the strict-mode flip.
